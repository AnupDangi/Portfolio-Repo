I really liked Zero to One. The book Zero to One tells us what it takes to build a company that goes from zero to one, which means creating something new instead of just making something that already exists a little better. I liked how Peter Thiel uses stories of companies that did well to explain why most startups do not succeed and what kind of mindset the founders of these companies need to have to build something that really matters.

There are three things from this book which I really liked.
These are going from zero to one understanding why most startups fail and the idea that competition is not what it is about. The book Zero to One says that of always trying to beat our rivals we should focus on building something that is one of a kind and playing by our own rules. Being successful is not about having a great idea it is about making good choices and getting better every day.

Reading Zero to One changed the way I think about building products. Now when I am building something I do not ask myself how I can make it a little better than what's already out there which he says 10x better. I ask myself if I am really creating something that's different from everything else. I think anyone who is interested, in startups or technology or building products that solve problems should read the book Zero to One.
