#### Preface

In late 2022, during my early days as a B.Tech student specializing in AI/ML, I witnessed the release of ChatGPT. Initially, many saw it as a tool to help write essays—useful, but perhaps not revolutionary. Few could predict the transformative role AI would soon play across industries.

Fast forward, we are now in an era dominated by advanced LLMs and autonomous agents. Autonomy has become central to the global economy, with optimization at its core. As someone closely following AI’s evolution, I’ve seen us move from the limitations of recurrent neural networks to the game-changing transformer architecture. Since the 2017 paper that introduced transformers, scaling LLMs has been the dominant path. But today, we’re nearing scaling limits.

What lies ahead is a shift toward true intelligence—not just from internet data, but real-world learning. We see this in robotics: systems are trained on real-world tasks, much like human hands performing activities. Reflecting on this progression—from past limitations to today’s agentic AI—guides us toward the next chapter. This post will explore exactly that.

*Note: These are personal thoughts and perspectives based on my observations.*

#### What Should Students Do Now?

##### Undergraduate Level:
In recent years, some students—particularly freshmen—have started to assume that mastering CS fundamentals is less important because AI can write code for them. There’s a misconception that, with tools like ChatGPT and autonomous agents, coding from scratch is obsolete. However, this mindset overlooks a crucial reality: coding is not just about syntax; it’s about articulating logical steps, problem-solving, and building deeper understanding.

I’ve already seen students skip foundational steps. For instance, a junior approached me about deploying a project that was nothing but a plain HTML file with inline JavaScript and a server side code within one file. He wanted to deploy it using a tool designed for modern frameworks (i.e., Vercel), unaware of the underlying structure needed. This highlights a broader trend: students are relying on AI without understanding the principles beneath.

Fundamentals like data structures, algorithms, and step-by-step logic are not obstacles; they are the core of good engineering. If you’re in your first or second year, dedicate one to two hours a day to practicing these fundamentals. Watch quality tutorials, write code by hand, and build confidence in your ability to create solutions from scratch without AI. Once you can transform your thoughts into algorithms independently, AI will become a powerful tool to complement your skills, not replace them. Develop the ability to learn and build fast.

##### Graduate Level:
While graduate-level students should focus on building deep technical expertise, the domain you choose is equally important. AI isn’t just a standalone field; it’s applied across domains like economics, biomedical research, mathematics, and more. Whatever domain you pick, I suggest moving from a “T-shaped” approach—deep in one area, broad in others—to a “U-shaped” approach. In other words, aim for depth in multiple domains. By combining expertise—AI plus another field—you can tackle unique challenges and create novel solutions. Ultimately, graduate students should cultivate the habit of going deep in more than one discipline, allowing for innovation at the intersection of fields. Consistent curiosity and interdisciplinary thinking will be key.

##### What Should Professionals of Any Task Do?
For professionals, continuous learning is essential. As AI evolves, it’s vital not just to watch from the sidelines but actively engage with emerging AI tools and concepts. Use AI in your daily work, experiment with new features, and stay informed on what’s being released. This isn’t about blindly adopting every model; it’s about understanding how AI can impact your specific field. Ask yourself: What does this model bring? How does it work? Is it relevant to my domain? The goal is to strategically adopt AI—enhancing your skills and staying ahead of the curve—while remaining thoughtful and adaptive in a rapidly changing landscape.

#### Upcoming Threats and Advances of AI
The future of AI is both promising and unpredictable. The advances—from improved productivity to breakthroughs in healthcare, economics, or creativity—depend heavily on how we, as users, apply it. AI can be a force for societal good, enabling innovation, efficiency, and meaningful change. However, threats emerge when AI is misused, whether for deepfakes, disinformation, tampering with documents, or even orchestrating sophisticated cyber threats. The line between benefit and harm depends on intent and oversight.

Thus, it’s not just about the technology; it’s about ethical use. Governments and AI companies must create robust compliance and regulations, ensuring transparency and accountability—tracking how AI is applied and for what purpose. Ultimately, AI is a powerful tool. It’s up to us to decide whether we use it to create progress or cause harm.

#### What Changes Will the New Agentic AI Paradigm Bring?
While current models excel at coding, writing, and creativity, the next leap in AI lies in cross-domain intelligence. Future agentic AI will go beyond task-specific fine-tuning or token prediction. Instead, we’ll see AI capable of transferring knowledge across domains, blurring lines between disciplines like economics, biomedicine, and engineering. This is more than fine-tuning; it’s about adaptable, multi-domain reasoning.

In this paradigm, AI agents will perform human-like tasks at scale. Imagine a future where companies deploy specialized AI agents—like an “AI Fiverr”—handling tasks on demand. These agents will be hired, paid, and managed like human contractors, but they’ll offer specialized, efficient output. We’ll also see more dynamic, personalized interfaces where AI-driven software adapts content and workflows in real time, reducing clutter and enhancing productivity. In short, we’re heading toward a future where AI agents are not just tools; they’re adaptable partners in reshaping how work is done.

#### Why Are Mass Layoffs Recurring?
Mass layoffs are driven by several factors, many tied to how companies are reshaping their operations. As AI-powered automation accelerates, businesses are reducing repetitive tasks—whether in software development, customer service, or internal operations. AI now handles tasks from auto-generated code to predictive analytics, allowing companies to optimize costs and streamline workflows.

In this shift, companies are often restructuring their workforce: hiring fresh talent with AI-era skills while letting go of roles that are now partially or fully automated. Economic factors, such as global market shifts or cost-cutting, also play a role. Ultimately, mass layoffs reflect how businesses are reorganizing around AI-driven efficiency, seeking both speed and cost optimization.

#### What Skills Should We Learn?
The skills you should develop depend on your interests and goals. The key is aligning learning with what excites you. If you’re passionate about a particular domain—be it creative arts, engineering, healthcare, or business—focus on how AI can enhance your work there. For hobbyists, AI can be a tool to bring innovative ideas to life.

Beyond domain-specific skills, consider two paths: First, cross-domain knowledge—understanding how to apply AI across multiple fields. Second, deep expertise—if you’re aiming to become a specialist, develop the ability to dive deep, research, and innovate. Ultimately, the most valuable skill is cultivating curiosity and adaptability, ensuring you can learn, grow, and apply AI to what matters to you.

#### The Downfall of Attention Span Due to Content Consumption — What Should We Do?
Attention span is critical for deep learning, complex problem-solving, and creative thought. However, with the rapid consumption of AI-generated content—be it articles, videos, or quick fixes—our ability to focus deeply is waning. While AI speeds up content creation, it often reduces emotional depth and thoughtful articulation. We’ve gone from engaging deeply with human writing—like thoughtful blogs or community-driven platforms—to quick AI-generated responses. This shift, while convenient, can diminish our capacity for deep focus.

The real issue isn’t AI itself; it’s how we engage with it. We must consciously manage our content consumption. Not every new tool or piece of content deserves immediate attention. In social media platforms, we must allow or disallow AI-generated content to reach us. These companies should build a layer for “plug-and-play” strategies, filtering out distractions and prioritizing what truly matters. By doing so, we protect our attention span and ensure we’re using AI as a tool for meaningful progress, not as a source of overwhelm. Let’s focus on depth, intentional learning, and purposeful engagement.

#### Who Is Succeeding in the AI Era — And Why?
In this AI-driven era, success isn’t about luck alone. Those thriving are the ones who embrace AI—learning and creatively applying it to solve problems, build projects, and augment their work. They adapt and use AI as a tool, not a threat.

On the other side, layoffs and struggles often stem from shifts in the market, company decisions, and the rapid pace of change. It’s not just the technology—it’s how we respond. Some companies may stumble as they evolve, and individuals can feel left behind. The key is in how we integrate AI into our work and life. Success isn’t just about AI—it’s about adapting, finding purpose, and using technology to enhance, not replace, our unique human strengths.

#### AI, Unemployment, and the Future of Universal Basic Income
The issue of unemployment and the potential introduction of universal basic income could become more pressing in the future, especially as more people are laid off. Without a stable income, many individuals might struggle to meet basic needs. In response, governments and AI companies might collaborate to implement policies where a portion of AI companies’ profits contribute to a basic income for those affected. While this could provide a safety net, it’s crucial to consider the social implications. If people rely solely on survival income without purpose, some may feel directionless, potentially leading to harmful behavior. On the other hand, if individuals find meaning—be it through creativity, community contribution, or new pursuits—this could foster positive societal outcomes. Without a sense of purpose, however, we may see increased stress and societal challenges. It’s essential to strike a balance between support and purpose.

#### What I Suggest?
In this era of rapid AI advancement, we truly don’t know if or when “superintelligence” will emerge, so it’s best to stay grounded. Rather than focusing on distant speculation, the practical path is to hone your interests and passions now, using AI to deepen your expertise. If you love a field, apply AI to it thoughtfully. Build depth—become someone who understands both the technology and the domain. By doing this, you’ll not only have an edge, but also the ability to shape how AI is applied—ensuring it’s used ethically, with respect to human values and policies. In short, focus on what you love, deepen your knowledge, and help steer AI responsibly.

#### Insights and Summary
In this AI era, the landscape is shifting rapidly. We’ve seen both opportunities and challenges—from new forms of automation to concerns about attention spans and job shifts. Success hinges on how we adapt. Those who thrive are embracing AI—learning it, applying it to their passions, and using it as a tool to innovate. Meanwhile, we should remain grounded, focusing on what we enjoy and building deep expertise.

In terms of domains, consider these examples you might add:
*   In healthcare, AI can assist with diagnostics, but human empathy and decision-making remain vital.
*   In education, AI can personalize learning, but teachers’ mentorship is irreplaceable.
*   In creative fields, AI can assist in design or writing, but human imagination shapes the narrative.

In closing, I want readers to ask:
What domain do I love? How can I partner with AI to make a meaningful impact? By combining technology with purpose, we can all find our place in this evolving landscape.
